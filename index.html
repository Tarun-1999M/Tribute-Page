A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

11th President of India

Here's a timeline of A. P. J. Abdul Kalam:

  • 15 October 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduates in Physics from Saint Joseph's College
  • 1960: Gains degree in Aerounautical Engeneering from Madras Instittute of Technology
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO form the DRDO
  • 1980: Developed first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1980s-1990s Chief of Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme
  • 1992-1999: Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO
  • 1998: Chief Project Coordinator for Pokhran II nuclear tests
  • 1999-2001 Principle Scientific Advisor to the Government of India
  • 2002-2007 11th President of India
  • July 27, 2015 Passed away after collapsing during a lecture at IIT Shillong
